// THE LESSON
"Urgent" is not the same as "skip the safeguards."
James acted in good faith under real pressure. He wanted to help. And he paid the price for a system that made it too easy to bypass safety checks in the name of speed.

Urgency is real. But security controls exist precisely for high-pressure moments — because that's when mistakes are most likely to happen.

The question isn't whether James should have been faster or slower. It's: why did your organization have an emergency protocol that allowed production deploys without review?
"Speed without safeguards is just a faster way to make catastrophic mistakes."
